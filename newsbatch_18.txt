Credit: WLTX JACKSONVILLE, Fla. — An incentive agreement for The Winn-Dixie Company is back on track for a May 12 vote by Jacksonville City Council after the company agreed to reverse course on closing a Harveys Supermarket on West 48th Street that instead would stay open at least three more years. The council's Finance Committee deferred a vote last week on the economic development agreement geared toward keeping The Winn-Dixie Company's headquarters in Jacksonville after residents said the closure of the Harveys store would create a food desert for residents who live in that area. The deferral spurred a new round of negotiations with Winn-Dixie. City Council President Kevin Carrico said he plans to discharge the revised legislation for a vote by the full council at its May 12 meeting. Carrico said JAX Chamber Daniel Davis told him about Winn-Dixie's agreement to keep the store open at 201 W. 48th St. for three years as part of the proposed economic incentive package. City Council member Nick Howland said Winn-Dixie would give City Council an update 18 months before the lease for 48th Street store ends in 2029 about the company's plans for the store. The agreement does not contain a guarantee the store will be open after the current lease expires. Winn-Dixie officials have said from a business standpoint, the store has had a "reasonably strong performance" and a key issue is being able to negotiate a lease rate that makes financial sense in the competitive supermarket industry. Winn-Dixie would use "commercially reasonable efforts" to get an extension of the lease for the West 48th Street store, an attorney representing the company said during a meeting convened by City Council member Ju'Coby Pittman on May 8.
