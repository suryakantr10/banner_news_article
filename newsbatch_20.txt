Grace Schumacher | FauquierNow Facebook Twitter WhatsApp SMS Email Print Copy article link Save Facebook Twitter WhatsApp SMS Email Print Copy article link Save Almost a year and a half after a fire forced Silver Branch Brewing to close its Warrenton location in December 2024, the brewery is welcoming customers back for a 10-week summer pop-up. Christian Layke, co-founder and CEO of Silver Branch, told FauquierNow he’s excited to get back up and running in Warrenton, even in a limited capacity. “It’s just been way too long since we’ve had a chance to be present,” Layke said in a Friday phone call. “We’ve done a couple of things like First Fridays and such, but we’ve just been excited to be able to get back in the community.” While Layke recognizes the pop-up on the brewery's patio isn’t quite as exciting as a full reopening, he hopes it can stir up some of the same feelings. “Hopefully it’s a way of getting close enough that the magic that was Silver Branch is back in some form,” Layke said. The summer pop-up will operate as close to normal as the business has managed since the fire, with Silver Branch beer on tap and food trucks or vendors coming by each weekend. Layke added that having patrons inside will make the experience “pretty close” to how it was before the fire. While the summer shop is a short-term way to get back in business in Warrenton, the brewery and restaurant remains focused on figuring out a long-term reopening plan. Among the final hurdles is pinning down plans to reinstall the kitchen, the lack of which necessitated food trucks and vendors for the summer pop-up.
