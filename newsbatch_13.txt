Title: Dunkin’ to open at Mill Run this week, offer first guests free coffee
Status hint: Opening | Industry: Specialty / Other

COLUMBUS, Ohio ( WCMH ) – A new Dunkin’ location at Mill Run in northwest Columbus near Hilliard will soon open. The coffee and donut fast food chain will begin welcoming customers at 3635 Data Point Court on Wednesday. The location will hold a grand opening celebration from 9 a.m. to 11 a.m., featuring “swag giveaways” and an appearance by Spinkes, the Dunkin’ mascot, according to a news release. The first 100 guests in line will also receive a voucher for a free medium hot or iced coffee, which will be valid for 100 days starting on May 6, according to a spokesperson for Gilligan Company, the franchisee of the Mill Run Dunkin’. At 10 a.m., the store will host a ribbon-cutting ceremony and check presentation for Columbus Humane, as part of the brand’s “ongoing commitment” to give back to the local community, the release said. A few adoptable dogs will be in attendance. In addition to the grand opening celebration, the Dunkin’ at Mill Run will operate during its regular hours on Wednesday. The store will be open from 5 a.m. to 7 p.m. daily. The Data Point Court building previously housed a Steak ’n Shake and has since been redeveloped to accommodate two tenants. A second business has not yet moved into the space. Dunkin’ – founded in 1960 in Quincy, Massachusetts – has about 10,000 locations nationwide, including more than 300 in Ohio. The chain serves a variety of hot, iced and frozen coffee drinks, along with tea, donuts, Munchkin donut holes, muffins, bagels, breakfast sandwiches, wraps and hash browns. The Mill Run development surrounding the new Dunkin’ has undergone several recent changes, including the addition of a luxury pet hotel and OhioHealth’s purchase of a newly built facility. A 120-unit apartment complex is also underway; Dune Companies broke ground on the project in October, and the first units are expected to be delivered this spring. The firm intends to complete the complex by the fall, according to NBC4’s previous reporting .

It has taken about seven years, but Jersey Mike’s Subs is opening in Exeter Township in just a couple of days. The new store location, in the Exeter Commons Shopping Center, will officially open on Wednesday, May 6, starting with a ribbon cutting ceremony with township officials and the Greater Reading Chamber Alliance. The new Jersey Mike’s is the third Berks County location for franchise-owner Gerrit Curran and will be his 27th location. The other Berks stores are in Muhlenberg and Spring townships. “Exeter is a great part of the community we haven’t been able to engage with yet,” Curran said in an interview. “We’ve been watching it for a while, and we finally had the opportunity to come in.” Curran said at about 2,000 square feet, the Exeter restaurant is “a little bigger than we otherwise prefer, but after five, six, seven years of waiting, we didn’t want to wait any longer.” The fast casual restaurant has a team of about 20, Curran said, adding that training began on Wednesday, April 29. Orlando Rosario is the location’s general manager. He is a three-year veteran of the company and previously worked at the Muhlenberg Jersey Mike’s.
