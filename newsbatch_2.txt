Another clothing store is leaving Prince Rupert as Warehouse One faces liquidation Warehouse One in Rupert Square Mall is expected to close after parent company Warehouse One Clothing announced plans to liquidate stores across Canada under creditor protection proceedings. The Winnipeg-based retailer, which also operates Bootlegger, has entered proceedings under the Companies’ Creditors Arrangement Act (CCAA) and plans to liquidate all 128 stores nationwide rather than restructure the business. If approved by the court on May 15, liquidation sales are expected to begin shortly afterward. The Prince Rupert store remains open for now. The company cited declining mall traffic, increased online competition and ongoing financial losses in court filings tied to the insolvency process.
